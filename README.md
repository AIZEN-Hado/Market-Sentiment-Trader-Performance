# 📊 Market Sentiment vs Trader Performance

## 🧭 Overview
This project explores the relationship between **Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear & Greed Index)** and **trader performance** using historical trading data.

The goal is to uncover patterns between market psychology and trading outcomes, and derive actionable insights.

---

## 📁 Dataset
- 📌 Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index (Market Sentiment)
- 📌 Historical Trader Data (Hyperliquid).

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
- 🐍 Python  
- 📊 Pandas  
- 📉 Matplotlib  
- 💻 Google Colab  

---

## 📈 Key Findings
- 🚀 **Extreme Greed** → Highest profitability (~238 avg PnL) and win rate (~57.7%)  
- ⚠️ **Extreme Fear** → Lowest performance (~0.58 avg PnL) and win rate (~28.7%)  
- 📊 Highest trading activity occurs during **Fear**, not necessarily profitable  
- 💰 Selling dominates in **Extreme Greed** → Indicates profit-booking behavior  

---

## 🧠 Insights
- 📈 Traders perform best in **strong bullish sentiment**  
- 😰 Fear-driven markets lead to **panic and inconsistent results**  
- 🔄 Market sentiment directly impacts **behavior and profitability**  
- ⚠️ Presence of extreme outliers suggests need for **data cleaning**

## 📌 Strategy Suggestions
- ✅ Focus trading during **strong bullish sentiment**  
- ⚠️ Avoid aggressive trades in **Extreme Fear**  
- 📉 Use controlled exits during **high-profit phases**  
- 🛡️ Prioritize risk management over high leverage
